Mountains located around Syracuse

There are thousands of different mountains you can ride around the country and even more around the world. Here in Syracuse, I will give you some selections for learning riders to experienced riders.

Labrador Mountain

This mountain is around 30-45 mins away from the university but is an excellent place for beginners to learn. It has a bunny hill, greens, blues, and diamonds and they are all great to learn on. With the mountain being at Syracuse and the weather we get here, there are many opportunities to get out there where the powder is fresh. They also have a nice lodge for you to warm up in after you get done snowboarding for the day

Greek Peak

This mountain is around 50 minutes away from the university and is great for experienced snowboarding. They do have terrains for people to learn on, but this is a much bigger mountain than Labrador. They usually have a great amount of snow covering the mountains and some woods riding if you are interested in that. If you are interested or like snowboarding in the terrain park, Greek peek has one to offer as well. They have many jumps, rails, and different boxes to try. They also have a lodge for you to warm up in and grab a bite to eat.

Bristol Mountain

This mountain is a bit farther than the other too, but worth the drive. This mountain is in Rochester NY and around an hour and 15 minutes away from Syracuse. This mountain is perfect for someone who has some experience In snowboarding but also is still learning. If you are interested in learning how to ride the terrain park, Bristol offers 3 different locations to practice on the mountains, and they host a yearly park jam where you can either compete or watch other people perform their best tricks. Bristol has 6 different lifts located on the mountain and 39 trails. If you are also looking to save money, every day after 3:30pm Bristol offers a Twilight ticket for only $35 dollars and gives you access to the mountain till close.

Favorite Mountains Located in The United States

Outside of Syracuse, there are many different mountains to explore. Two mountains that I found to be the best and my favorite are Vail and Jackson Hole. Both mountains are more advance but have great training lessons offered if you were looking to learn there.

Vail Mountain

Vail is located in the Rocky Mountains of Colorado and is around 2 hours out of Denver. Vail is known for being one of the best mountains in the country due to its size and the amount of snow that falls every year. The mountains are spread out over 5 different faces and offer up to 273 different trails to explore. They also have 30 different lifts and 2 gondolas to get you around the mountain. While exploring the mountain you will be able to find many different lodges and restaurants scattered around the faces. The mountain is located in vail village which has all sorts of things to do as well as a great nightlife to enjoy after a long day of snowboarding. If you are looking for more of a challenge or just like to adventure, I highly recommend checking Vail out

Jackson Hole Mountain

Jackson hole is very similar to vail but is in the neighboring state, Wyoming. Jackson Hole is mostly known for its extreme summit drop where professionals try to take it on every day. Jackson Hole has around 130 trails with 13 different lifts to explore. Similar to vail you will find many different lodges and restaurants scattered around the mountain. Id recommends this mountain for the more skilled snowboarders as it has a lot more black diamonds and double black diamonds compared to the average mountains. If you are ever in the area I highly recommend that you check it out, and even if you aren't the best snowboarder it's still a site to see!
